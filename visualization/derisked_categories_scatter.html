De-risked Climate Biotech Categories: Government Deployment Funding
Categories with abnormally high deployment funding ratios indicate technologies
considered proven/de-risked enough for government deployment-stage support
Anomaly Detection Method:
Deployment ratio (%) = (Deployment $ / Total $) × 100
Anomalies identified when Z-score > 1.5 standard deviations above mean
(i.e., deployment ratio > mean + 1.5×σ)

Statistical Summary:
Mean deployment ratio: 14.1%
Standard deviation: 23.7%
Anomaly threshold: >49.6% deployment funding

Important Caveat:
Category-level deployment ratios indicate overall de-risking trends but do not mean every technology within a category is equally mature, nor does this analysis capture every climate biotech category out there. High deployment ratios typically reflect specific proven pathways receiving federal deployment support, while novel or emerging technologies within the same category may still be in research phases. Always consider individual technology readiness when interpreting these results.
